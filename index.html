How prevention affect individuals' insurance choices? An experimental study


Abstract:

We propose an original lab-experiment to analyse the effects of introducing a risk prevention mechanism on individuals' insurance choices. After choosing a level of coverage under a compulsory insurance contract, the insured can make a costly prevention effort to reduce the likelihood of a loss occurring (self-protection). Our study shows that the possibility of a posteriori prevention lowers the level of coverage chosen by policyholders, thus revealing their intention to take precautionary actions to mitigate their risk. However, we find a dynamic inconsistency of choices: individuals who voluntarily chose to be less insured when prevention option is available do not subsequently engage in more prevention effort afterwards.
